WE ARE DPN132.

And this is our project, Misinformation Regarding Leni Robredo’s Personal Life, that focuses on misinformation and disinformation regarding ex-Vice President Leni Robredo and her personal life.

Leni Robredo, the Philippines' previous Vice President and one of the more prominent presidential candidate in the 2022 elections, has been a very polarizing figure on the Internet. She has been admired for her advocacy work and progressive views and hounded by critics for several reasons, including her political statements, alleged association with communist rebels, and personal life.

As disinformation shared through social media has become more prevalent over the years, we must become more vigilant in determining if the information being shared is correct. This is especially true for information regarding Leni Robredo, as numerous accounts had shared disparaging personal mis/disinformation to ruin her credibility as a politician.

PROBLEM FORMULATION

Research Objective

To determine the most common mis/disinformation regarding Leni Robredo and her personal life.

Hypotheses

There are no relevant themes to the tweets regarding Leni Robredo's personal life.

Action Plan

Analyze the topic of tweets that post mis/disinformation about Leni Robredo and her personal life.

Null Hypotheses

There are relevant topics that are discussed in mis/disinformation regarding Leni Robredo's personal life.

MATERIALS AND METHODS

Our methodology is as follows:

Data Collection

We have collected a total of 141 valid tweets for this study. Some original tweets from the dataset were deleted as they were outliers, were unrelated, or had missing values. We mostly collected tweets about Leni Robredo's personal life, which can be found using related keywords on Twitter.

Data Pre-processing

Some issues in the dataset, including missing and/or invalid values and outliers were dealt with accordingly. NLP techniques such as tokenization and lemmatization were incorporated to prepare the dataset for the machine learning model.

Data Exploration

We mainly checked the frequency of keywords used to search tweets and the distribution of topics over time to generate additional insights.

Data Modeling

Topic clustering using Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) was done for the study.

Statistical Modeling

For statistical modeling, we used Bayes Factor for Hypothesis Testing.

DATA PRE-PROCESSING

Missing and/or invalid values

The usual columns that have missing or invalid values are the Location and Bio columns. In order for us to visualize the number of Location and Bio values, missing values will be handled by categorizing them as ‘No Location’ or ‘No Bio’ or ‘No Bio and Location’. Invalid values Tweets with posting dates after 2022 were deleted.

Outliers

Outliers were removed in the dataset using pandas. Z-score standardization was used in order to identify the outliers in each numerical column in the dataset.

Formatting consistency

Since the data was obtained manually (without a scraper), we ensured to follow the format specified for each column. However, this method is prone to human error. Some methods that we have used to further verify the format of the available data include the conditional formatting function of Google Sheets.

Normalization/standardization/scaling

We have mainly used normalization and standardization to identify outliers. Z-score standardization was used as mentioned before.

Categorical data encoding

Some of the columns that are considered categorical data are the Content type and Account type columns. For the content type column, we have equated 1 for Emotional, 2 for Rational, and 3 for Transaction. On the other hand, we have equated 1 for Identified, 2 for Anonymous, and 3 for Media.

Natural language processing techniques

To further process the dataset for machine learning, several techniques in natural language processing were utilized. Lowercasing was done with the String library in Python. Aside from lowercasing, removal of emojis, emoticons, and hyperlinks were also done using the same library. Tokenization, stemming, and lemmatization. and stop word removal was done with the Natural Language Toolkit suite. Lemmatization was mostly done to the remaining words in the dataset to retain their meaning and ensure more accurate clusters.

DATA MODELING

For this study, we used topic clustering and statistical analysis to evaluate the dataset that we have created and to test the resulting insights against our hypothesis.

TOPIC CLUSTERING

For topic clustering, we used Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), which is a Bayesian model that gives a document a specific topic based on the words it contains. We decided with four topics as we wanted to see as it provided more consistent themes based on the tests that we have done. Moreover, these four topics were consistent with four major themes that have appeared in our data exploration: Aika Robredo’s rumored sex tape, her alleged affair with Bolet Banal, her said involvement with Jesse Robredo’s assassination, and Butch Robredo’s alleged drug trade.

  • Topic 0: drug, lord, brother, butch, naga, brother-in-law, jesse, city, law, protector
  • Topic 1: bolet, banal, affair, fake, child, said, make, relationship, married, supporter
  • Topic 2: husband, drug, killed, wife, lord, bolet, first, npa, hiding, know
  • Topic 3: video, sex, said, look, like, bolet, good, also, son, aika

STATISTICAL MODELING

Due to the subjective nature of the topic, the lack of readily available quantitative data and overall difficulty of finding "unbiased" data, we were unable to employ standard statistical tests, such as t-tests or chi-square tests, which require a standardized level of confidence. Consequently, we opted to utilize Bayesian factor analysis for hypothesis testing, as it allows for the evaluation of subjective evidence in favor of or against a hypothesis based on prior beliefs and observed data.
The prior probability for this project is assumed at 0.25. The posterior probability is then calculated by getting the likelihoods of each word by using the formula (likelihood = (1 / math.sqrt(2 * math.pi * sigma**2)) * math.exp(-(x - mu)**2 / (2 * sigma**2))). The Bayesian Factor is then calculated by dividing the posterior probability by the prior probability.
Using the Bayesian Factor for hypothesis testing we have the following:
Bayesian factor < 1, fail to reject h_0
Bayesian factor >= 1, reject h_0
Then we have the Bayesian Factor for each topic:
Topic 0: 1.1490791980975832
Topic 1: 1.4238397602032118
Topic 2: 1.1347613804834755
Topic 3: 1.1649544469999507
As observed, since the Bayesian factors for all topics are all greater than 1, we reject the null hypothesis for all topics and thus accepting the alternative hypothesis and accepting these 4 topics as a topic for discussion about leni robredo's personal life.
To add, a greater Bayesian factor implies that the words in each topic have a higher posterior probability; implying that each topic is more connected each other. Additionally, if the sample size were to be increased, the effect would likely enhance the statistical power of the analysis. A larger sample size provides more data points, making the posterior probability more precise and reducing uncertainty and increasing the precision of the estimated Bayesian factors.

RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

By applying LDA modeling for topic modeling and clustering analysis, along with Bayesian factor for hypothesis testing, we identified four relevant clusters within our dataset. This approach not only provided insights into the underlying topics but also quantified the strength of evidence for the presence of these clusters. Based on the findings that we have, there are some implications and insights that can be drawn:

  • A more generalized data collection method (e.g. the use of more general keywords in manual/automatic data collection) might be beneficial to draw more accurate clusters, with more underlying topics available in said clusters.
  • Some of the clusters have topics that are not generally known by the public, namely the said involvement of Butch Robredo in the illegal drug trade in Naga City and the alleged affair between Leni Robredo and Bolet Banal.
  • The presence of topics such as affairs and sexual videos in mis/disinformation regarding Leni Robredo suggests that she and her daughters are targeted victims of misogyny, which is a consistent theme during her candidacy in the 2022 Elections.
  • Sentiments regarding the drug war are still strong as ever, represented by the strong presence of mis/disinformation about Butch Robredo.

There are also some insights that we have taken note of about the dataset:

  • Some tweets regarding the alleged assassination of Jesse Robredo were related to the assassination of Ninoy Aquino and Cory's said involvement, showing the long-standing contempt against the Aquino family and the parallels between Leni and Cory.
  • Other topics include Leni's blood relations with the NPA (including farfetched news about Leni and Jose Maria Sison's "romantic relationship"), Leni's said first husband, and Jesse's said affairs.

There is an increasing need to verify information in the Internet about Leni Robredo's personal life, especially since some of them are way beyond rationality (e.g. Butch Robredo's disability may as well prevent him to becoming heavily involved with Naga's drug trade). These existing 'fake news' against Leni Robredo also strengthens negative sentiment against other political figures that are related to her in any way, which makes it difficult to mitigate. There is also available insight in this study that can be related to several themes in our society today, including misogyny against female political figures, sexual taboo, and the general sentiment supporting the war on drugs. Therefore, we must be vigilant on consuming these topics, counter the reinforcement of negative sentiments and challenge the perpetuation of false narratives, as they perpetuate harmful stereotypes and prevent the advancement of progressive thought in our society.
